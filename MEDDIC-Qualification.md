# MEDDIC: Enterprise Sales Qualification Framework

## Overview

MEDDIC is a comprehensive opportunity qualification methodology used by enterprise sales organizations to assess the viability and closability of large deals. It helps sales teams identify red flags early and focus on truly qualified opportunities.

**MEDDIC stands for:**
- **M**etrics
- **E**conomic Buyer
- **D**ecision Criteria
- **D**ecision Process
- **I**mplication of Problems & Impact
- **C**hampion

---

## 1. Metrics (Business Value & ROI)

### Purpose
Quantify the business impact and value of the solution.

### Key Questions to Answer
- What are the specific business metrics that matter to this prospect?
- What measurable outcomes would success look like?
- How would you quantify the value of solving this problem?
- What KPIs would improve if this solution was implemented?
- What's the financial impact (revenue, cost savings, efficiency gain)?

### Assessment Criteria
✅ **Strong Signal**: Buyer has specific metrics in mind; can articulate ROI

❌ **Red Flag**: Buyer is vague about value; can't quantify impact; decision is subjective

### Example Metrics
- Time savings per user per month
- Cost reduction percentage
- Revenue opportunity enabled
- Efficiency gains in percentage or hours
- Error reduction percentage
- Compliance risk mitigation value

### Qualification Checklist
- [ ] Specific, quantifiable metrics identified
- [ ] Buyer has done preliminary ROI calculation
- [ ] Metrics align with organizational priorities
- [ ] Financial impact is material enough to justify action
- [ ] Metrics track can be established post-implementation

## 2. Economic Buyer (Budget Authority)

### Purpose
Identify who controls the budget and has final sign-off authority.

### Key Questions to Answer
- Who ultimately approves this budget?
- If there were no constraints, who would make the final decision?
- Who is accountable for the results of this solution?
- Does this person have proven experience committing similar budgets?
- What is their motivation for seeing this project succeed?

### Assessment Criteria
✅ **Strong Signal**: You have direct access to or clear path to economic buyer; they've committed to timeline

❌ **Red Flag**: Economic buyer is blocked; unclear who has real authority; budget hasn't been allocated

### Important Distinctions
- **User**: The person who will use the solution day-to-day
- **Coach/Champion**: Your internal advocate who helps navigate the org
- **Influencer**: Technical or departmental specialist with input
- **Economic Buyer**: Person with budget authority and accountability

### Qualification Checklist
- [ ] Economic buyer identified by title and name
- [ ] Their role requires accountability for this solution area
- [ ] Direct path to economic buyer established (or clear next step)
- [ ] Economic buyer has discussed timeline with you
- [ ] Budget authority confirmed (not just assumed)
- [ ] Economic buyer understands business case

## 3. Decision Criteria

### Purpose
Understand the specific evaluation framework the buyer will use to make their decision.

### Key Questions to Answer
- What factors will you evaluate this solution against?
- Are you comparing multiple vendors or just evaluating fit?
- What must the solution do (must-haves vs. nice-to-haves)?
- What evaluation process will you follow?
- What are your deal-breakers?
- When will you need to make a decision?

### Assessment Criteria
✅ **Strong Signal**: Buyer has documented criteria; your solution maps clearly; criteria favor your approach

❌ **Red Flag**: Criteria are undefined; they heavily weight capabilities you lack; unclear evaluation timeline

### Two Levels of Decision Criteria

**Explicit Criteria** (What they tell you about)
- Technical requirements
- Pricing constraints
- Timeline requirements
- Implementation scope

**Implicit Criteria** (What they don't say, but influences decision)
- Organizational politics
- Personal preferences of influencers
- Vendor relationships
- Risk tolerance
- Technical debt/legacy system constraints

### Qualification Checklist
- [ ] Explicit criteria documented and shared
- [ ] Your solution meets must-have criteria
- [ ] Nice-to-have features differentiate you positively
- [ ] You understand implicit/political criteria
- [ ] Evaluation timeline is defined
- [ ] You can influence criteria (or criteria already favor you)

## 4. Decision Process

### Purpose
Understand the sequence and timeline of buying activities.

### Key Questions to Answer
- Walk me through your buying process step by step
- Who needs to be involved in each stage?
- What approvals must happen before purchase?
- What are the key gates and decision points?
- What's your realistic timeline?
- Are there any seasonal or budgetary constraints?
- Has this type of purchase been made before? How long did it take?

### Assessment Criteria
✅ **Strong Signal**: Clear, defined process; realistic timeline; aligned with your sales cycle

❌ **Red Flag**: Vague process; timeline keeps slipping; decision process longer than typical; uncertainty about approval gates

### Typical Enterprise Decision Process Stages

```
Initial Interest
    ↓
Internal Assessment / Budget Allocation
    ↓
Requirements Definition
    ↓
RFP/RFQ (if applicable)
    ↓
Vendor Evaluation & Demos
    ↓
References & Due Diligence
    ↓
Legal & Contract Review
    ↓
Executive Approval
    ↓
Purchase Order / Commitment
```

### Qualification Checklist
- [ ] Entire buying process mapped and documented
- [ ] Key stakeholders identified for each stage
- [ ] Approval gates identified
- [ ] Timeline for each stage estimated
- [ ] Timeline is realistic and achievable
- [ ] Your internal sales process aligns with their buying process
- [ ] No unexpected hurdles anticipated

## 5. Implication of Problems & Impact

### Purpose
Quantify the business pain and urgency of solving the problem.

### Key Questions to Answer
- What's happening today because this isn't solved?
- What's the business impact of the status quo?
- How is this problem affecting revenue, costs, or efficiency?
- What happens if this problem persists for another quarter/year?
- How is this problem affecting your team's ability to perform?
- What competitive risk does this create?

### Assessment Criteria
✅ **Strong Signal**: Buyer has felt the pain; can articulate tangible impact; urgency is real

❌ **Red Flag**: Problem is abstract or theoretical; low urgency; buyer is just "exploring options"

### Building the Implication Case

**Level 1: Awareness of Problem**
- "We're aware we could be more efficient"

**Level 2: Understood Impact**
- "This is costing us approximately $500K annually in lost productivity"

**Level 3: Emotional Commitment to Fix**
- "This is jeopardizing our ability to compete; we must fix this this year"

**Level 4: Specific Buying Intent**
- "We've decided to solve this and are evaluating vendors this quarter"

### Qualification Checklist
- [ ] Specific problem identified and acknowledged by buyer
- [ ] Business impact quantified or estimated
- [ ] Problem affects strategic initiative or key metric
- [ ] Multiple people recognize the problem's importance
- [ ] Buyer has emotional (not just intellectual) commitment to fix it
- [ ] Problem severity creates real urgency

## 6. Champion (Internal Advocate)

### Purpose
Identify a powerful internal advocate who will champion your solution.

### Key Questions to Answer
- Who believes strongly in solving this problem?
- Who has credibility and influence across the organization?
- Who will advocate for you when you're not in the room?
- Do they have a personal stake in the solution's success?
- What's their relationship with the economic buyer?
- How will they benefit from this change?

### Assessment Criteria
✅ **Strong Signal**: Champion has power/credibility; genuine belief in solution; able to influence economic buyer

❌ **Red Flag**: No champion identified; identified champion has no influence; champion's motives are unclear

### Champion Characteristics
- Has direct access to economic buyer
- Understands internal politics and how to navigate them
- Has credibility with other stakeholders
- Personally benefits from the solution succeeding
- Willing to provide internal references and feedback
- Proactively advocates (doesn't just answer questions)

### Champion Development Strategy

1. **Identify**: Find someone with problem awareness and influence
2. **Engage**: Share education and business case materials
3. **Align**: Ensure their personal objectives align with solution value
4. **Empower**: Provide them with talking points and support
5. **Activate**: Ask them to champion internally; provide updates
6. **Support**: Stay engaged; address concerns quickly

### Qualification Checklist
- [ ] Champion identified by name and title
- [ ] Champion has direct access to economic buyer
- [ ] Champion understands business case for solution
- [ ] Champion's personal success is tied to solution success
- [ ] Champion has credibility and influence in the organization
- [ ] Champion is actively advocating (not just aware)
- [ ] Champion can articulate why solution is needed

## Red Flags & Deal Killers

### Immediate Red Flags ⛔
- Economic buyer doesn't believe in value proposition
- No identified champion
- Timeline is unrealistic for your sales cycle
- Deal criteria explicitly favor competitors
- Budget has been pulled due to business conditions
- Problem won't be addressed until next fiscal year

### Proceed with Caution 🔶
- Economic buyer is hard to reach
- Metrics haven't been quantified
- Multiple viable solutions being considered
- Internal politics suggest significant obstacles
- Champion lacks real influence
- Timeline keeps slipping

## Using MEDDIC in Your Pipeline

### Early Stage
- Focus on understanding Metrics and Impact
- Identify initial Champion
- Understand scope of Decision Criteria and Process

### Mid Stage
- Develop Champion relationship
- Quantify Metrics and Impact further
- Get clarity on full Decision Process
- Identify Economic Buyer

### Late Stage
- Direct Economic Buyer engagement
- Refine Decision Criteria to align with your solution
- Detailed timeline confirmation
- Champion advocacy maximization

## MEDDIC vs. Other Qualification Frameworks

| Framework | Best For | Strength | Limitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MEDDIC** | Enterprise, long cycles | Comprehensive | Can feel prescriptive |
| **BANT** | Simple deals | Quick, easy | Limited depth |
| **SPICED** | Sales engagement focus | Modern approach | Less emphasis on economics |
| **MEDDPICC** | Extended MEDDIC | Adds competition | Complexity can slow adoption |
